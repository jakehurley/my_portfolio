This study applied data processing techniques to analyze violin harmonic spectra. We unveiled unique resonance patterns and emphasized the impact of playing techniques. Our comparative analysis highlighted bowing as a technique significantly enriching harmonic spectra. Quantitative evaluation of harmonic amplitude decay, facilitated by Fourier Transform data analysis, provided crucial insights into sustained sound quality. Notably, the Stradivarius replica exhibited a decay rate of 5.7 (±0.6) × 10-9, surpassing the entry-level violin at 2 (±2) × 10-8. This data-driven approach underscores the intricacy of violin design and playing techniques, guiding musicians and enthusiasts toward instruments with better sonic qualities.
